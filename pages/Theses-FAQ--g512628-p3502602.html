Proof of completion for future employers

  • Once your thesis has received final approval from the Grad Office, both you and the registrar's office will receive an automated emailfrom CaltechTHESIS indicating your thesis has been approved.  Once the registrar has noted the final thesis approval in your REGIS record, you should be able to access and download, within REGIS, a PDF copy of a letter of proof of degree completion.  This letter can be sent to future employers.

Citation Format and Linking for Theses

  • This citation format is derived from the Chicago Manual of Style (14th ed.).  We have merely added the Resolver URL in these examples:
    • Pauling, Linus Carl (1925) The Determination With X-Rays of the Structures of Crystals. Dissertation (Ph.D.), California Institute of Technology. doi:10.7907/F7V6-4P98. https://resolver.caltech.edu/CaltechETD:etd-05142003-141307
    • Painter, Oskar Jon (2001) Optical Nanocavities in Two-Dimensional Photonic Crystal Planar Waveguides. Dissertation (Ph.D.), California Institute of Technology. doi:10.7907/y7hm-zq23. https://resolver.caltech.edu/CaltechETD:etd-12282005-111605
  • Use either the DOI or the Resolver URL as a permanent link to your thesis.  Do NOT use the link from your browser's search box, as it may become obsolete as the hardware and software used for CaltechTHESIS change.
