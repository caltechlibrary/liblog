Databases

  • Beallslist.net: Beall's List of Predatory Journals and Publishers
    "Predatory, open-access publishers ... exploit the author-pays model of open-access publishing (Gold OA). ... Operating essentially as vanity presses, these publishers typically have a low article acceptance threshold, with a false-front or non-existent peer review process ... add little value to scholarship, pay little attention to digital preservation, and operate using fly-by-night, unsustainable business models.
  • Beall's List: Criteria for Predatory Open-Access Publishers
    Based on an analysis of publisher content, practices, and websites according to established ethical standards.
  • BioMed Central
    All 'Research' articles are free to access. Arthritis Research and Therapy, Breast Cancer Research, Critical Care and Genome Biology and Genome Medicine also publish review articles which are accessible on a subscription basis. Journal supplements and Meeting abstracts published by BioMed Central are freely accessible.
  • CERN: Where to publish
    Provides listing of HEP journals and information on article charges.
  • Databib: Reserach Data Repositories
    "Databib is a searchable catalog / registry / directory / bibliography of research data repositories." Entries can be browsed alphabetically or from 20 broad subject categories. Similar sites include: DataCite [https://datacite.org] and Open Access Directory: Data Repositories [https://oad.simmons.edu/oadwiki/Data_repositories]
  • Digital Commons Network
    A foundation for free exchange of scholarly work, including journal articles, bok chapters, dissertations, working papers, etc.
  • Edanz Journal Selector
    A journal finder that is currently free and offers an option to limit the search to only journals with Open Access options.
  • Elsevier Journal Finder
    "Journal Finder uses smart search technology and field-of-research specific vocabularies to match your article to Elsevier journals." There is also an option to "Limit to journals with Open Access options".
  • EPJ Web of Conferences
    EPJ Web of Conferences provides open-access proceedings from the whole spectrum of pure and applied physics.
  • Free Medical Journals
    Ranked (Impact Factor) listings of journals with free access: Immediately, After 1-6 months, After 7-12 months, and Later ... and other filter options.
  • Gallica
    A digital archive maintained by the French National Library. Has late 19th and early 20th century science journals as full text PDFs.
  • Highjacked Journal List
    "Updated List of Hijacked Journals and Fake Publishers by Dr. Mehrdad Jalalian"
  • Internet Archive
    "The Internet Archive ... is building a digital library of Internet sites and other cultural artifacts in digital form."
  • JANE: Journal/Author Name Estimator
    Enter title or abstract keywords and get a list of appropriate (PubMed) journals which can be filtered on Open Access titles.
  • OA-publishers AND Print on demand in a grey zone
    "There are Open Access publishers that operate in a grey zone and you should therefore think twice before you submit an article to such a publisher."
  • OAIster Database
    Contains 23M records of digital resources from more than 1.1K open-archive collections worldwide.
  • Paperity
    "Paperity - a multidisciplinary aggregator of Open Access journals and papers". Currently provides access to the full text of 171,228 papers.
  • QOAM = Quality Open Access Market
    "Quality Open Access Market is primarily for authors who want to publish their article in open access in a high quality journal and for a reasonable price." Apparently are not aware of Beall's List?
  • ROAD, the Directory of Open Access Scholarly Resources
    "ROAD provides a free access to the subset of ISSN bibliographic records identifying online scholarly resources available in Open Access: journals, conference proceedings and academic repositories. These records, created by the ISSN Network (88 ISSN National Centres worldwide + the ISSN International Centre), are enriched by information about the presence of the scholarly resources concerned in indexing and abstracting databases, registries and journals indicators sources."
  • ScienceOpen
    ScienceOpen is a searchable database of Open Access content from a wide variety of publishers … that can also be browsed under various Discipline headings. Full text articles from Biomedicine fields, for example, are imported from the PubMed Central Open Access Data Subset starting from the year 2000, and arXiv papers in Physics, Mathematics, Computer Science, Quantitative Biology, Quantitative Finance and Statistics are included beginning with 2011.
  • Stop Predatory Journals
    These journals and publishers cheapen intellectual work by misleading scholars, preying particularly early career researchers trying to gain an edge.
  • Web of Science - HighWire - Free Online Full-text Articles
    "As of 3/25/15, we are assisting in the online publication of 2,434,604 free full-text articles and 7,659,003 total articles. There are 65 sites with free trial periods, and 166 completely free sites. 295 sites have free back issues, and 1508 sites have pay per view!"
  • Who's Afraid of Peer Review? Bohannon's List
    Publisher Data page for the results of a 2013 study based on the submission of 304 fake research papers to open access journals. The papers were designed with such grave scientific flaws that they should have been rejected immediately by editors and peer reviewers.
