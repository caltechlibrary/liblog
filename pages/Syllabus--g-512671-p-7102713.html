The Vikings

Syllabus

This course will look at these Scandinavian seafaring warriors of the eighth through the eleventh centuries as an historical problem. What were the Vikings, where did they come from, and how did they differ from the Scandinavian and north German pirates and raiders who preceded them? Were they really the horned-helmeted, bloodthirsty barbarians depicted by modern popular media and by many medieval chronicles? What effect did their roughly two centuries of raiding and colonization have on the civilizations of medieval and ultimately modern Europe?

Readings : The readings for this course blend primary sources with secondary scholarship on Vikings. The following texts are required:

1. Beowulf , trans. Ringler

2. Carolingian Chronicles , trans. Scholz

3. Egil’s Saga , trans. Scudder

4. Saga of the Volsungs , trans. Byock

5. The Vinland Sagas , trans. Kunz

6. King Harald’s Saga , trans. Magnusson

In addition, we will be reading materials that I will provide to you as handouts. These readings are given in the course schedule below.

Grading : The course focuses on the reading, analysis, and discussion of the material listed above. Class participation and attendance will therefore make up 40% of your grade . There will be no midterm and no final examination. Instead, I will ask you to write three papers on issues concerning Vikings at select points during the course. These papers will together make up the remaining 60% of your grade.

In addition, I will ask you to write short summaries/reaction papers before each discussion meeting. These papers MAY BE NO LONGER THAN one page long. They should include a BRIEF summary of the essential points or essential content of the reading(s) and a BRIEF statement of your reaction, i.e., things you found interesting, questions you had, things you agreed/disagreed with, etc. These papers will help me set up and guide the discussions. Please send these papers to me VIA E-MAIL. They must reach my in-box BY 10.00 AM ON THE DAY OF THE RELEVANT DISCUSSION. In accordance with their purpose, I will not grade these papers as I would essays; instead, they will form part of your attendance/participation grade.
