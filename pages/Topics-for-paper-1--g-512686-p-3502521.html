Topics for paper I

H 192 – Crusades

Winter 2012

TOPICS FOR PAPER I

Answer one of the following questions in a paper of at least 2000 words (note: please double-space so that I have room for my comments):

1. Using all the sources from weeks one through three [NB: through section on Crusader Palestine] that you think appropriate, tell me what you think the motive(s) were for the First Crusade. If you think there were several, which motive applies to which group within the Crusaders? Or is it even possible to make general statements? [note: it might (or might not) help you to look at the four accounts of Pope Urban II’s speech at Clermont, in the Crusades reader starting on p. 39]

2. Using all of the sources from weeks one through three that you think appropriate, tell me why you think the First Crusade succeeded.

3. Do you see in the Gesta Francorum any echoes of the Song of Roland ? If so, what are they? Does the Gesta help us decide to what degree Roland in fact reflects the European aristocracy’s mental world around the time of the First Crusade? Or (if you want to tackle the question this way), does Roland in fact help us understand the mind of the Gesta’s anonymous author, and/or the First Crusade more generally?

4. One of the eyewitnesses to the First Crusade was one Raymond d’Aguilers, a chaplain of Raymond of Toulouse who accompanied the count on the crusade. Raymond d’Aguilers later wrote a Historia Francorum qui ceperunt Iherusalem (History of the Franks who conquered Jerusalem); unlike other major writers on the crusade, however, he did not draw on the Gesta Francorum . I have put Raymond d’Aguilers’ Historia on reserve in SFL. Read it up through the end of chapter XIV. How is it the same and how does it differ from the Gesta ? Can you come up with an explanation (or some explanations) why?

5. Fulcher of Chartres was also a participant in and eyewitness to the First Crusade; he was part of the entourage of Count Stephen of Blois and Robert of Normandy, and became chaplain to Baldwin of Boulogne (who became Baldwin of Edessa). Fulcher wrote his own chronicle of the First Crusade, the Historia Iherosolymitana or History of the Expedition to Jerusalem , which draws not only on his own observations but also on both the Gesta Francorum and Raymond d’Aguilers. I have likewise placed a copy of Fulcher’s chronicle on reserve. Read Book I up to chapter XXIX. Compare it with the Gesta Francorum . How does Fulcher’s chronicle differ from the earlier work, in its understanding of the Crusade, its understanding of the principal figures and their motives, and in any other respect that you think is significant?

6. Read either Raymond d’Aguilers or Fulcher of Chartres as above. Would your chosen text serve better than the Gesta Francorum as the representative Frankish account of the First Crusade for this course? Why or why not? [note: BE SPECIFIC. “I liked it better” (for example) won’t fly].

If you have a different question about that you want to explore in a paper, come see me.
