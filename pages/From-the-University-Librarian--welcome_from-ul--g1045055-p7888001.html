Dear Caltech Community,

Welcome back to campus for Fall 2021!  Caltech Library spaces and services are open to serve you, though they won't all look the same to you as they did for our last Fall term on campus.  We've moved some things ( TechLab ), we've created some new services (DIBS) , we've developed new partnerships ( coming soon Vroman's @ Caltech), and we've instituted some pandemic-driven safety requirements ( study space reservations .) Our mission, however, has not changed, and supporting the research and teaching of the Institute remains the top priority of the Caltech Library.

Below-- and throughout this guide-- you’ll find information on our current services, and we encourage you to review our offerings and use them to benefit your work. Please note that all Library spaces are open to current Caltech ID holders only and N95, KN95, KF94 or similar masks must be worn at all times in all indoor Library locations.

Library materials may be returned at Sherman Fairchild Library or any of the orange Caltech Library bookdrops you see around campus (books only) or the SFL service desk/window (for books, media, and equipment). For those not able to visit campus, Library materials may also be returned via USPS prepaid shipping. Contact circ@library.caltech.edu for more information.

DocuServe services are available. All document delivery and Interlibrary Loan (ILL) requests will be fulfilled in digital form wherever possible. DocuServe will scan articles and book chapters from items currently in the collection if requested but will not scan whole volumes. As always, members of the Caltech community may request items not held at Caltech Library via our Interlibrary Loan service. If items are unavailable via ILL, the request will be referred to Subject Librarians for purchase evaluation for the collection.

All of the Caltech Library's numerous online services remain fully available , including reference and research consultations for the Caltech community (email: library@caltech.edu , archives@caltech.edu ) and access to all electronic resources ( Library Guides , electronic books and journals , databases , etc.). Please note that if you are accessing our electronic resources remotely, refer to our guidelines for Off-Campus Access .

Wishing you well,
Kara Whatley, University Librarian
