What Is UnPaywall?

Unpaywall is a new Web discovery tool that looks for free, legally-uploaded online PDF copies of paywalled articles that it harvests from thousands of open-access repositories worldwide. Developed and provided by the not-for-profit organization ImpactStory, Unpaywall aims to make science more open and reuseable. Unpaywall is currently in pre-release: it is scheduled for official release in April 2017.

Testing by the Caltech Library indicates that the tool is still buggy and does not always find open access copies of subscription articles when we know they exist. But it does work some of the time, and that is encouraging ! We recommend adding Unpaywall to the Open Science toolkit for continued comparison with other discovery tools  -- this is a rapidly evolving landscape!

How to Use Unpaywall

Unpaywall works as an extension for the Chrome web browser.  As you are looking at the web landing page for a subscription (paywalled) article, Unpaywall will be checking its database for an open access version of that work and indicate availability using color-coded tabs as shown below.

Once installed as a Chrome extension, you click the green Unpaywall extension icon in your browser toolbar and choose "options." Once there, tick "Color-code tab for Green and Gold OA."

  • Gold tab for open access articles available from the publisher under an open license.
  • Green tab for open access articles on a preprint server or institutional repository.
  • Blue tab for articles available on the current page, but lacking license information (often that's because you're browsing from behind the paywall).

Example of Unpaywall at Work

In the example here, a user without a subscription to Geology needs to get a copy of a paper published in that journal.

When browsing  the landing page for the article on the publisher’s web site (in Chrome), the Unpaywall extension detects a match in its database of free articles. Clicking on the green flag takes the user to a free copy of the paper. In this case, Unpaywall found an open access copy of the article that was uploaded by the authors to the CaltechAuthors repository. The publisher allows posting of the authors' final accepted manuscript to open access repositories.

Example of Unpaywall not working

In the example here, a user without a subscription to Geology is hoping to find an open access copy but Unpaywall is unable to detect a match in its database of free articles. That may mean there is insufficient data available about possible free copies, or it may mean that no open access version exists.

Unfortunate, but not the end of the line: the Caltech Library is able to order a copy of the article through DocuServe !
