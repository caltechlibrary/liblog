Writing for the Web

Most people don't read closely on the web. Instead, they skim over content, searching for the information they need. Writing should be consistent, clear, and concise to best meet user needs. When in doubt, refer to The Chicago Manual of Style .

Less is more

  • Limit the number of pages, boxes, bullets, widgets, and other content to around 5 items.
  • Avoid using subpages as these are often overlooked.
  • Limit descriptions to 1-2 concise sentences, especially when describing databases and links.
  • Use who, what, where, why, how to direct your writing.
  • Cut or condense unnecessary introductions or welcome messages.

Be human

  • Lose the formality and use common, less complex words ("get" instead of "obtain"; "buy" instead of "purchase").
  • Tailor content to your audience (public vs. faculty vs. staff etc.)
  • Use active voice over passive voice ("We provide study spaces." vs. "Study spaces are provided at the library.")
  • Use pronouns. The user is You. The library is We. ("We provide study spaces you can use by yourself or in groups.")
  • Avoid jargon. Use words that the audience will understand. ("The link resolver will direct you to intermediary pages with direct links to publisher and vendor-provided sites with pdf or html versions of articles." vs. "Select 'View It' to see full-text articles.")

    • Caltech Hall Library?
    • Geology Library
    • Dabney Library
    • Cahill Library
    • Caltech Archives & Special Collections on first mention, then the Archives

Resource types

  • journal not ejournal
  • catalog not catalogue
  • ebook (Ebook when capitalized)
  • database not research database or online database
  • library guide (only refer to LibGuides internally with library staff)

Online terms

  • web not Web, website not web site.
  • email not e-mail (Email when capitalized)
  • Internet / internet : Capitalize as a noun (i.e. "on the Internet") but not as an adjective (i.e., "internet resources")

Time and date

  • 8:00 am–5:00 pm
  • Monday–Friday
  • March 1, 2022
  • 03-01-2022

Terms to avoid

  • digital or electronic or e-* : Patrons–especially students–expect most resources to be available on the Web.
    • "Online help is available through our chat reference service." vs. "Visit our chat reference service for help."
  • library or libraries : Visitors will assume you are talking about the library and its resources, so no need to repeat these terms.
    • "Library study rooms are available on the fifth floor." vs. "Study rooms are available on the fifth floor."
    • "Find this book in the library." vs. "Find this book."

Capitalization

Capitalize most words, with the exception of articles and some prepositions, also known as title case. Refer to Chicago Manual of Style.

Exception: Quick Links should be written in sentence case (no period)

Numbers

Written as words:

  • numbers one through ten
  • numbers that begin a sentence
  • numbers that are adjectives (thirty-first)

Naming Conventions

Subject Guides

  • Title: subject or topic of guide only (Biology and Biological Engineering)
    • Don't use extra words such as guide, resource, information, tools, learning, or about.
  • Friendly URL: shortened guide title only, with hyphens (libguides.caltech.edu/open-access)

Course and Other Guides

  • Title: Course prefix: Course title (Wr1: First-Year Composition)
  • Friendly URL: course prefix (https://libguides.caltech.edu/wr1)

Friendly URLs

  • Balance brevity and clarity. Use as few words as possible that can clearly identify a page
  • Use hyphens between words.
  • Not every page needs a friendly URL. Feel free to leave the automatically generated URL in place.

Punctuation Standards

  • Do not use an ampersand (&) in place of the word "and" except on rare occasions in a title.
  • Use the Oxford comma (comma after the next to last item in a series before the conjunction).
  • Single space after periods at end of sentence (as opposed to double spaces).
  • Quotation marks should be double with single quotation marks used only for quotes within quotes.
