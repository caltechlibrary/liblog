Caltech Library Government Documents

Caltech does not have a separate and discrete publicly-accessible U.S. government documents collection. Caltech is selective and integrates the material into the overall collection and in recent years puts priority on electronic resources.

For assistance with obtaining government information:

  • Use this guide
  • Submit your question . We will respond as soon as we can.
  • Text us: 626-587-4833
  • Search for an answer to your question in our FAQ.

California Government Resources

  • Official Voter Information Guide
  • CA.gov
    State of California government portal
  • California State Budget Documents
  • California's Governor's Office
    Official website of the current governor.
  • California State Online Directory
  • California Roster
    Listing of California's public officeholders, including U.S. government officials, Constitutional officers, members of the State Senate and Assembly, Judicial branch, county officials, and incorporated city and town officials.
  • State Agency Database
    Annotated list of databases produced by the State of California
  • California State Agency Directory
    Alphabetical list and a subject index to all California state agencies
  • California Legislative Analyst's Office
    Office ensures the executive branch implements legislative policy in an effective manner, by analyzing the operations and finances of state government. Full text publications are available as well as analysis of ballot propositions from 1996.
  • Public Policy Institute of California (PPIC)
    As a nonprofit, nonpartisan think tank, Public Policy Institute of California (PPIC) is dedicated to informing and improving public policy in California through independent, objective, nonpartisan research.
  • California Ballot Measures Database
    The California Ballot Measures Database is a comprehensive, searchable source of information on California ballot propositions and initiatives from 1911 to the present. It includes the full text of individual ballot propositions, accompanying information contained in the ballot pamphlets, related legal and legislative history, and pdfs of the full ballot pamphlets. For ballot initiatives, it contains the full text of the individual initiatives, accompanying material relating to their filing & qualification, related legal and legislative history, and pdfs of related official documents.
  • California Research Bureau
    Provides nonpartisan research on California issues and legislation.
