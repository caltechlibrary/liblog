June 13, 2022

SPIE Digital Library - The Library subscribes to the SPIE Digital Library. Unfortunately the linking between ADS and SPIE content is taking campus users to a Docuserve menu. We absolutely pay for this content and our eresource librarian is working on fixing these links. In the meantime I suggest contacting me or library@caltech.edu if you're having trouble with linking. You shouldn't encounter issues if you're on campus or using VPN. As well, you can manually create a proxied link to content by stitching together the library's proxy address + the DOI. Here's an example:

https://caltech.idm.oclc.org/login?qurl=https://doi.org/10.1117/12.2557565

https://caltech.idm.oclc.org/login?qurl= | The Library's proxy server

You can add this prefix to any DOI suffix, no spaces, to force your browser to navigate through the proxy server.

Web of Science & Caltech Connect links - There is currently an issue with WoS links to our licensed resources via the Caltech Connect button. 90% of the time the Caltech Connect button does pass you through to the article. 10% of the time it does not resolve and you get dropped off at an EBSCO link resolver page with a pale yellow bar at the top and a message "If this page does not display, then open the page in a new window". If you click on the blue "...open the page in a new window" all should be well. The alternative, in WoS is to click on the " Full Text at Publisher " link to the right of the Caltech Connect button. This way you'll avoid the EBSCO error page. Apologies for this hassle.

AAS page charges and OA - With 2022 the American Astronomical Society journals have become Open Access (OA). The Library no longer subscribes to the AAS journals, including Astrophysical Journal, Astronomical Journal ++. Some people on campus have noticed that AAS article charges have gone up with this change. These journals now rely 100% on author charges. The AAS no longer receives any revenue from library subscriptions.

April 4, 2022

Astronomy & Astrophysics [journal] & S2O - EDP announced today that subscriptions have allowed this journal title to move to an Open Access model in 2022. The model is called Subscribe to Open (S2O). Newly published content in A&A will be published under a CC-BY 4.0 license allowing authors to retain copyright. Libraries will need to continue paying subscriptions to EDP in order for this model to continue beyond 2022. To learn more about this please see the full announcement and the EDP press release .

Oxford University Press ebooks - Our Library has several hundred ebooks from Oxford University Press.
