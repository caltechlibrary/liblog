Literature Search Preparation Exercise

Identify Key Concepts and Descriptions

Read over the notes that you've taken so far. Think about and note what you think are the key terms you chose. These could be the names of people, significant events or places, or time periods. They can also include scientific or social concepts, including processes or changes. Write down as many as you can - try to get at least three.

Identify Related Terms

Looking at the terms you've identified, are there other terms, synonyms, or descriptions that might be related to them? Try to identify at least two for each term. You should have a total of at least nine terms.

Searching!

Databases may cover different journals and different time periods. It's important to think of several other related terms to use in your search. Databases may use different key terms in their indexing. In addition, terminology may have changed over time - so a concept may be referred to by different names at different times in history.

Read over the Recommended Databases page for tips on accessing articles using the CaltechConnect button and on evaluating the appropriateness of the sources you find. Then, using the database list on that page, try formulating some searches using the terms you've identified. Follow these guidelines:

  • Keep a record of what database you use and your specific search query.
  • Choose a minimum of three databases and three search queries. Run each query in each database - you should have a minimum of nine groups of search results.
  • If you decide to change your search query, keep track of what you change and if your results get more or less relevant.
  • You may find the resources listed on the Finding References tab, particularly the Boolean Operators section, helpful.
  • Take a minute to compare the results you get and write notes on which query and database combination(s) seem to give you the most relevant results.
