It's Getting Easier to Find Free (and Legal) Copies of Peer-Reviewed Research!

There are approximately 34,000 scholarly peer-reviewed journals published globally, collectively publishing over 2.5 million articles a year (International Association of Scientific, Technical and Medical Publishers, 2015). Of this number, an estimated 27-55% are available on a free-to-read basis (ibid.; Archambault, E et al, 2014).

Free copies of articles may reside on the sites of the journal publishers themselves; on a scholarly sharing platform where authors post copies of their works to share with others; or in a digital repository that archives pre-published versions of submitters' research works. In all cases, the research is free to read. The ability to reuse and redistribute these works, however, varies according to the copyright status of the work and the distribution license associated with it.

This Guide shares tips and tools for finding free pee-reviewed research articles on the Web. This information may come in handy when you are looking for an article that the Caltech Library does not subscribe to.

Please also keep in mind that Caltech library users may request copies of published articles from the DocuServe service, usually at no cost to you. The DocServe guide provides additional information about that service, turnaround times, etc.
