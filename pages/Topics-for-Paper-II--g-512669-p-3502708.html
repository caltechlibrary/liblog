H 109 - Medieval Knighthood                                                                                                              Fall 2012

TOPICS FOR PAPER II

Answer one of the following questions in a paper of no less than 2000 words, drawing on material from both the relevant readings and the lectures as you think necessary:

  1. The Miracles of St. Foy and Thietmar of Merseburg’s Chronicon are roughly contemporary. Are the milites (=warriors/horsemen) of the Miracles the same creatures as the milites of the Chronicon? In other words, do you think milites have developed similarly or differently in the worlds projected by the two texts?

  1. Assume the translator of Fulcher of Chartres History of the Expedition to Jerusalem is correct in translating milites/equites as “knight.” What does Fulcher think a knight is? [Note: it will help you to answer this question to read Book II]

  1. Does Galbert of Bruges' Murder of Charles the Good reveal a knightly culture of violence in early twelfth century Flanders? In other words, does Galbert describe characteristic uses of violence that help define who is a knight, who is not, and whether one is a good or a bad knight? If so, does this culture of violence have anything to do with religion? (Hint: among other things, you will have to deal with Galbert's point of view - you will be getting into the issue of what you can say about the external "reality" of chivalric culture in early 12c. Flanders from within one person's narrative).
