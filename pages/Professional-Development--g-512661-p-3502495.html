Resources at Caltech

  • The Hixon Writing Center at Caltech
    The Hixon Writing Center has professional tutors, under the direction of Dr. Susanne Hall, to help you at any stage in the writing process. It is located on the third floor of the Center for Student Services building.
  • Center for Teaching, Learning, & Outreach
    The Center for Teaching, Learning, and Outreach supports Caltech's multifaceted educational efforts, including undergraduate and graduate courses and curricula, formal and informal learning, and partnerships with PreK-12 teachers and students.
  • The ACS Guide to Scholarly Communication
    Updated in 2020, the Guide provides students, researchers, educators, and librarians with the instruction and advice they need to master the art of scholarly communication. This Guide covers everything from classic journal authoring topics (now completely modernized) such as Organization of Your Article and Peer Review, to styling conventions needed for all scholarly communication, such as Chemical Nomenclature and Effective Writing and Word Usage, to emerging areas of interest in scholarly communication, including Open Access & Open Science, Sharing Data, Communicating Safety Information, and Preprints.

Textbooks / Teaching Resources

  • National Center for Case Study Teaching in Science
    Over 400 carefully constructed case studies that can be searched or browsed by topic.
  • UC Davis Chemwiki
    ChemWiki is a Open Access supplementary textbook that is written and re-written by both students and faculty members and is directed by Prof. Delmar Larsen. A freely available account is required to edit the site modules.
  • Wired Chemist
    Provides students and professionals with instructional information ... with tabs to chemistry, mineralogy, environmental and NMR, which are further subdivided into instructional, data and links.
  • Virtual Textbook of Organic Chemistry
  • Textbook Revolution - Chemistry
    Textbook Revolution provides access to free educational materials with links and reviews of textbooks and select educational resources, as PDF files, e-books, or web sites containing course or multimedia content.
  • Youtube EDU - Chemistry
    Freely available chemistry courses
  • DeskTopper version of the Alexander Arrangement of Elements
    "The DeskToppers are the quick and easy form of the Alexander Arrangement of Elements (AAE), especially useful in a full classroom, hands-on lesson just prior to the lesson in your curriculum that normally introduces the periodic table." (from website)
  • Elements as Characters
