Celebrating Women's History Month

Links to educational and informational sites, podcasts, and activities.
  • Women's History Month on History.com
    "Women’s History Month is a celebration of women’s contributions to history, culture and society and has been observed annually in the month of March in the United States since 1987. Women’s History Month 2023 will take place from Wednesday, March 1 - Friday, March 31, 2023"
  • American Women: A Guide to Women's History Resources at the Library of Congress
    The Library's unique and diverse collections are rich with resources for the study of women's history. This research guide gathers together a collection of topical guides and essays that tell the stories of women in America.
  • Women in Science: 50 Fearless Pioneers Who Changed the World
    "This fascinating, educational collection features 50 illustrated portraits of trailblazing women in STEM throughout history."
  • Encyclopedia Womanica
  • Hysteria. WOMEN: 51% OF THE POPULATION AND 100% OF THIS PODCAST.
    Political commentator and comedy writer Erin Ryan and former White House Deputy Chief of Staff Alyssa Mastromonaco are joined by a bicoastal squad of funny, opinionated women to talk through everything from reproductive rights to romcoms. They break down the political news of the week, plus the topics, trends, and cultural stories that affect women’s lives. New episodes drop every Thursday.
  • 36 Activities To Celebrate Women's History Month (2023)
    A list of links. Podcasts, organizations, and more.
  • National Women's History Alliance
    "The National Women’s History Alliance, which spearheaded the movement for March being declared National Women’s History Month, has announced the women’s history theme for 2023, “Celebrating Women Who Tell Our Stories."
  • Under the Sisterhood
    "The space for women to be seen, heard and celebrated."
  • BBC Radio 4 Women's Hour
    BBC Podcast.
  • What's Her Name Podcast
    "What’sHerName is a global women’s history podcast hosted and produced by academic sisters Dr. Katie Nelson and Olivia Meikle. We tell the stories of the amazing women World History left out. Every episode features one woman, centered on an interview with a guest historian, writer, or scholar."
  • Beyond Curie
    "A newsletter of notable women, nonbinary, and trans people in the history of science and medicine, written by author Olivia Campbell. Each newsletter will highlight a different person whose impact on science should be much more widely recognized."
  • Civil Rights Queen: Constance Baker Motley and the Struggle for Equality
    "The first major biography of one of our most influential judges--an activist lawyer who became the first Black woman appointed to the federal judiciary--that provides an eye-opening account of the twin struggles for gender equality and civil rights in the 20th century."
  • Women in the History of Science: A sourcebook
    Women in the History of Science brings together primary sources that highlight women’s involvement in scientific knowledge production around the world.

Happy Birthday, Albert Einstein!

Please see last March's post for suggested reading material!
  • Einstein Papers Project
    The Einstein Papers Project is engaged in one of the most ambitious scholarly publishing ventures undertaken in the history of science. The Collected Papers of Albert Einstein provides the first complete picture of Einstein’s massive written legacy.
  • Albert Einstein Archives
    The Albert Einstein Archives contains the largest collection of original manuscripts by Einstein in the world and includes his vast correspondence with the most influential physicists and intellectuals of the 20th century. Moreover, it comprises the most exhaustive compilation of material about Albert Einstein.
  • Einstein: The Man and His Mind
    "An unprecedented visual biography of the iconic pioneer of modern physics, with signed photographs, letters, manuscripts and more."
