Atomic Spectra

  • Ground Levels and Ionization Energies
    Periodic table format for neutral atoms
  • Handbook of Basic Atomic Spectroscopic Data
    Wavelengths, intensities, and spectrum assignments for neutral and singly-ionized atoms of all elements hydrogen through einsteinium (Z = 1-99).
  • NIST Atomic Spectra Bibliographic Databases
    Transition probabilities, spectral line broadening and energy levels and wavelengths.
  • NIST Atomic Spectra Database
    1. Spectral lines and associated energy levels displayed in wavelength order with all selected spectra intermixed or in multiplet order. Transition probabilities for the lines are also displayed where available. 2. Energy levels of a particular atom or ion displayed in order of energy above the ground state.

Citing Spectra

  • ACS Citation Style for spectra
    "References to data retreived from a database should be cited like a web site and include the CAS Registry Number (RN) or chemical name and the date the database was accessed. If the data are calculated data, also cite the software used for the calculation."

Molecular Spectra

  • Carotenoids handbook
    Contains UV spectra for ~700 compounds
  • NIST Chemistry WebBook
    A majority of spectra are taken from original scientific papers. Some are taken from published collections.
  • SciFinder-n @ Caltech
    REG file records may have literature references. Substance record references can be filtered by 'Spectral Properties'.
  • Sigma-Aldrich Handbook of Stains, Dyes and Indicators
  • UV atlas of organic compounds
  • Spectra Data Base System for Organic Compounds (SDBS)
    SDBS is an integrated spectral database system for organic compounds,which includes 6 different types of spectra under a directory of the compounds. The six spectra are as follows, an electron impact Mass spectrum (EI-MS), a Fourier transform infrared spectrum (FT-IR), a 1H nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectrum, a 13C NMR spectrum, a laser Raman spectrum, and an electron spin resonance (ESR) spectrum.
