ETDs freed here

The following North American institutions of higher education have adopted policies that allow graduate students to decide whether or not to submit their dissertations to the commercial reseller, ProQuest/UMI (meaning they’ve gone “ProQuest Optional” or “NoQuest”). This type of policy represents a significant break from the status quo established in the Microfilm Era of the mid-20th Century, recognizing the possibilities and power of the Digital Era of the 21st Century.

Note the number of heavy-hitters on this list in terms of graduate research, as reflected by their common Carnegie Classification as Comprehensive Doctoral degree granting institutions (either CompDoc/MedVet or CompDoc/NMedVet). Many are also members of the prestigious Association of American Universities (AAU) with top-tier research libraries belonging to the Association of Research Libraries.

This list continues to grow — in fact, a number of additional institutions have alerted this LibGuide aiuthor of their intentions to change policies soon. So please stay tuned and feel free to add a comment to let us know of any schools we’ve missed!

  • Boise State
    The Graduate College no longer sends copies of the dissertation to ProQuest. If you’d like to submit your dissertation to ProQuest, contact ProQuest directly (https://www.proquest.com/en-US/products/brands/pl_umidp.shtml).
  • Brown University
    Brown ETDs will be available in the Brown Digital Repository (BDR). A permanent URL will be available for use in your resume. Dissertations of candidates who elect to enter into an individual publication agreement with ProQuest will also be available in the commercial ProQuest Dissertations and Theses Full Text database.
  • Caltech
    Effective Fall 2015, Caltech changed its thesis submission process to require submission only to the library-hosted Caltech Thesis system
  • Carnegie Mellon
    Dissertation Checklist for Electronic Submission gives student option to select or decline submission to ProQuest
  • Florida International University
    On the Final ETD Approval form, Doctoral students can opt to let UGS submit their dissertation to ProQuest.
  • Georgia Tech
    Guidelines specify submission to the GaTech Digital Repository only.
  • University of Tennessee - Knoxville
  • University of Texas - Austin
    When uploading your dissertation to the Texas Digital Library submission site, you may choose to have a copy of your approved dissertation sent to UMI for supplemental publication, preparation of bound copies, and registration of copyright.
  • University of Southern California
    Guidelines specify submission to the USC Digital Library only.
  • University of Massachusetts - Amherst
    NoQuest policy affirmed in posting to mailing list scholcomm@lists.ala.org on 12-1-2015.
  • University of North Texas
    The Toulouse Graduate School web site states "ProQuest submission in Vireo is optional."
  • Worcester Polytech
    “Special Procedures and Fees for Dissertations: WPI no longer requires that Ph.D. Dissertations be microfilmed by University Microfilms, Inc. (UMI).”
  • University of Victoria
    In April 2014, the contract between Library and Archives Canada and ProQuest (formerly University Microfilms International) came to an end. Up until this date, students were required to send their ETD to ProQuest for publication in the Dissertations and Theses (ProQuest) database. The Faculty of Graduate Studies decided to discontinue this requirement, which not only saved the students some cash, but was also considered unnecessary, since submission of ETDs to UVicSpace gives students exposure through Google, Google Scholar, WorldCat, and in addition, LAC harvests UVic's ETDs and makes them available via the Theses Canada portal.
