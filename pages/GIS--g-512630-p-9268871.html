QGIS

QGIS (sometimes referred to as Quantum GIS) is a free and open-source Geographic Information System that can be downloaded and installed by anyone.

It is available on a variety of platforms, including Windows, Mac, and Linux.

For beginners, we recommend the standalone installer. The latest version will have the newest features, while the previous version is more stable. We recommend the long term release (more stable).

Download and install from QGIS's web site .

QGIS is also installed on all our Library computers, including the Geology Library, the Map Room, and the Sherman Fairchild Library.

Basemap and Digital Elevation Data Resources

  • ASTER Global Digital Elevation Model (DEM)
    Provides a global digital elevation model (DEM) of land areas on Earth.
  • Astrogeology Science Center (USGS)
    DEMs and remote sensing for various planets and moons.
  • The National Map (USGS)
    DEM's, hydrography, and National Land Cover dataset from various federal agencies.
  • Natural Earth
    Integrated vector and raster data.
  • NOAA DigitalCoast
    Geospatial data and satellite imagery related to coastal resources and management.
  • OpenDEM
    OpenDEM is a public resource for contributing and sharing elevation data from around the world.
  • OpenTopography
    DEM's, remote sensing, raster data, and topography resources for the U.S. and selected areas around the globe.
