Review Schedule

Content owners receive reminder 2 weeks before each term starts to review and update content as needed per the web content standards.

All edits are published by the start of term.

  • Fall term - review in early September

  • Winter term - review in mid-December before break

  • Spring term - review in mid-March

  • Summer term - review in early June

News/Blog Standards

Unpublish news stories more than one year old (unless content is still relevant) so they don’t flood Google results with outdated information.

Standards Checklist

Guides are updated four times a year before the start of each term. Use the following checklist to guide your edits:

Getting Started

  • The Side-Nav Layout is used.
  • Guide has been assigned the right type (e.g., Topic Guide).
  • Guide title is clear, follows naming convention, and is used in friendly URL. Guide includes a useful description

Layout & Style

  • Pages, boxes, and lists are limited to 3-5.  If there are more than 5 items, headings are used to divide the list into sub-categories.
  • No subpages are used.
  • The most important content is at the top of the page/box/list.
  • Paragraphs of text are broken up into lists or structured with headings, and kept short and simple.
  • Default font, color, and formatting are used.

Language, Terms & Naming

  • Writing is friendly, specific, concise, consistent, and jargon-free.
  • Standard terms are used for common words (e.g., TechHub not Tech Hub).
  • Naming conventions for guide titles (e.g., Accounting) and friendly URLs (e.g., libguides.caltech.edu/americanhistory) are used.

Assets, Images & Video

  • Assets or reusable content were used to create content and links when reasonable.
  • All links have been checked for content and function. (Find broken links: Tools > Link Checker > sort by owner to see a list of broken links in your guides.)
  • All content has been checked for currency and accuracy, and is tailored towards the appropriate audience(s).
  • No other content is in the left-hand column under the sub-menu.
  • Databases and links have short descriptions that are displayed below the title.

Accessibility

  • Guide accessibility has been checked using the WAVE tool (wave.webaim.org).
  • Content is written in plain language with a clear heading structure.
  • Videos and images with text have captions.
  • Tables are used for data not layout. If used for data, tables are accessible.
  • Links are meaningful and include alt text that describes the content and function.
