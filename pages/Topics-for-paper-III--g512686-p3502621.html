TOPICS FOR PAPER III

Answer one of the following questions in a paper of at least 2000 words (note: please double-space so that I have room for my comments):

1. Looking across the course, do you see any evolution in how the Crusaders/Frankish settlers in Palestine fought? Do you see any changes over time in strategy, tactics, logistics, siege technology, fortifications, etc.? If so, how might you explain the changes you see? If not, how do you explain the lack of change? [Note: be very careful to take the nature of your sources into account, i.e., what purposes or biases their authors might have had that might have led them to highlight or gloss over relevant information, or present it in certain ways. Is it possible that any patterns you see are more apparent than real?]

2. Looking across our sources for the entire period we have covered: do our Frankish authors seem to have developed any greater understanding of their Muslim foes between the late eleventh and the late thirteenth centuries? Or are any apparent differences in how Muslims are presented simply artifacts of the different identities, purposes, and perspectives of our authors?

3. Answer question number 2 for our Muslim authors.

4. A very few modern Christian groups still defend the Crusades as holy wars and present them as positive episodes in the history of western Christianity. As best I can tell, however, mainstream opinion, both within Christianity and without, regards the Crusades as a tragic blot on the history of the church, when Europeans perverted Christian teachings to justify a brutal assault on the Muslim peoples of the Middle East. What do you think? Having spent a term reading some of the most important primary sources on the Crusades, should we condemn them? Or can we excuse and perhaps even defend them on the grounds that they made sense and were justifiable within the mental, moral, and religious framework of the medieval society that produced them?
