Layout

Pages & Columns

  • Keep pages to a maximum of 7 to reduce cognitive load for our users.
  • Avoid adding content to the small column available under the side-nav menu to keep the page from becoming cluttered.
  • Additional column advice
  • Avoid creating sub-pages as these add unnecessary complexity to the menu and navigation.

Boxes

  • If your list contains more than 7 items, organize it into sub-categories using headings.
    • How to add headings: Click the edit icon at the bottom right of your box, then select Format from the gray toolbar, then Heading 3 or Heading 4.
  • Break up paragraphs of text into bulleted or numbered lists, or 1-2 sentence paragraphs separated by headings.
  • Place the most important content at the top of the page or list. Keep important content "above the fold" of the webpage (the part of the page that is visible without scrolling down).

Tables

  • Tables are meant for tabular content and data, not layout or design. When tables are used to create rows and columns for layout, they often do not resize correctly on tablets and mobile devices.
  • If you need to add a table to your box, please make sure it complies with accessibility requirements​ .

Accordions

  • Accordions should be used sparingly and only when there is enough content to justify the extra clicks. Read detailed accordion advice from Nielsen Norman Group.
  • Consider embedding a LibAnswers FAQ widget or using a text block with section headings as alternatives.

Font Styling

  • Follow Caltech web writing standards for colors and fonts .
  • Stick to system defaults for color, shape, size, font, and style to ensure consistency and legibility.
  • Left-aligned text is preferred because centered text can be difficult to read.
  • When adding a link, link text rather than pasting the raw link ( Reserve a study room vs. Reserve a room at https://libcal.caltech.edu ).
