This course is designed to introduce you to the history of Europe between 1000 and 1400. It will survey the economic, social, political, and religious evolution of the region during this period, with a focus on France, England, Italy and Germany.

Readings: the readings for this course consist of primary sources. There are three required books. The first two are: The Poem of the Cid, and Jean Froissart, Chronicles. The third is a collection of sources that we will dip in and out of throughout the quarter: Patrick J. Geary ed., Readings in Medieval History  (referred to below as Geary, with the name of specific selections). In addition to these three books, you will also occasionally get readings in the form of handouts.

Course requirements and grading: The course is built on the reading, analysis, and discussion of the material listed above. Class attendance and participation will therefore make up 20% of your grade. In lieu of a midterm, you will write two papers of at least two thousand words each on one or more of the readings. I will provide paper topics in advance. If you have questions of your own that you would like to pursue, however, you may do so; please clear your topics with me. The two papers together will make up 40% of your grade. A final examination, likewise making up 30% of your grade, will end the course.

In the seventh week we will hold a formal debate in class, to decide once and for all the proper relationship between religion and government and in particular between Church and Empire. Your preparation and participation in this debate will make up the remaining 10% of your course grade.
